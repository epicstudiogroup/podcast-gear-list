Podcast Preparation

Host Gear & Setup Guide

CEO-Authors Podcast

To ensure a broadcast-quality production, we have curated a list of essential equipment and environmental requirements. Please review this checklist to prepare for your interview.

The Camera

A dedicated camera ensures you look sharp and professional.

Recommended Standalone Webcams

  • Logitech Brio 500 Excellent 1080p quality with superior auto-light correction. The standard for modern home offices. (~$130)
  • OBSBOT Tiny 2 Lite or Meet 2 4K resolution with AI tracking that keeps you centered in the frame. Very sharp image. (~$150)
  • Anker PowerConf C200 The best budget option. 2K resolution and crisp clarity for the price point. (~$60)

Mac User Pro Tip: Continuity Camera

If you have an iPhone (XR or later) and a Mac, you do not need to buy a webcam. Use the "Continuity Camera" feature to use your iPhone's rear camera as a webcam. It provides quality superior to almost any webcam on the market. You will simply need a cheap MagSafe mount (e.g., Belkin) to attach it to your monitor.

The Audio

Good audio is more critical than good video.

Recommended Microphones

These mics look professional on-camera and capture broadcast-quality voice.

  • Shure MV7+ (USB/XLR) The industry standard for high-end podcasting. It rejects background noise and makes any voice sound rich. Connects directly via USB-C. (~$280)
  • Rode PodMic USB A dynamic broadcast microphone that looks fantastic on video. Extremely durable and offers studio-quality sound via USB. (~$200)
  • Blue Yeti X The classic choice. Versatile, easy to use, and reliable. Ensure it is set to "Cardioid" mode (heart symbol) to capture only your voice. (~$130)

Technical Requirements

Fast Computer

Video recording is resource-intensive. A slow computer will cause audio drift (lip-sync issues) or freezing.

  • Processor: Apple M1/M2/M3 chip OR Intel Core i7 (10th Gen or newer).
  • RAM: 16GB Minimum (8GB is risky for video recording).
  • Storage: 512GB SSD (Solid State Drive) minimum.

Stable Internet

Stability is more important than raw download speed. Use a wired Ethernet cable if possible.

  • Test Site: Use speedtest.net before the call.
  • Upload Speed: Must be above 10 Mbps (Critical for sending your video).
  • Jitter/Ping: Ping should be under 20ms; Jitter under 30ms.

The Suit

Specific Suggestions

  • Colors: Navy blue, charcoal grey, or deep slate. These contrast well with most backgrounds and skin tones.
  • Solids over Patterns: Wear solid colors. Avoid tight stripes, checks, or herringbone patterns, as they cause a "shimmering" effect (moir√©) on video.
  • Contrast: Ensure your suit jacket contrasts with your background. (e.g., Don't wear a navy suit against a dark blue wall).

The Background

Physical Setup (No Virtual Backgrounds)

  • Depth: Pull your desk 3-5 feet away from the wall behind you. This creates natural depth and looks high-end.
  • Content: A bookshelf with books, a small plant, or clean tasteful art works best. Avoid clutter or open doors/hallways.
  • No Blur/Virtual: Please disable "Blur Background" or virtual images. They often glitch around hair and hands, lowering the production value.
